The Credential: Legitimizing "Tech for Good"

How we ensure the certificate provides real resume value for STEM students rather than just being a piece of paper.

1. Project-Based Portfolios

STEM recruiters care about what students actually build. To earn this certificate, students don't just sit and listen; they must complete a "Tech for Good" mini-project during the workshops.

Actionable Output

Examples: Building a basic webpage about UNICEF's clean water initiatives, or creating a simple data graph mapping education inequality.

Direct LinkedIn Value

Students can link these official UNICEF-themed projects directly to their GitHub or LinkedIn profiles as proof of technical extracurricular volunteer work.

2. Official Endorsements

A certificate gains legitimacy based on who backs it. We will design a professional template that establishes it as a recognized university extracurricular achievement.

Signed by Leadership

Co-signed by the UNICEF Faculty Advisor, Club Presidents, and the STEM Director.

3. Strategic Co-Sponsorships

To maximize resume prestige, we can invite our guest speakers or the Computer Science/Physics departments to "co-sponsor" the credential.

A certificate featuring the UNICEF logo alongside a guest organization's name (e.g., DataKind) is a massive draw for ambitious students.
